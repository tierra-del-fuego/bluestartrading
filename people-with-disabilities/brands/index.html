Designing Inclusive
and Accessible
Brands for All.

Our Actions

We work to make people’s everyday lives better by designing accessible products, packaging, and communications that make it easier and more convenient for everyone to use the products they love. We focus on accurately reflecting and representing all consumers in our advertising to authentically reach and resonate with the communities we serve.

Inspiring Accessible Innovation

The positive impact we’re making to support people with disabilities comes through in our branded products and packaging. We build many of the world’s most trusted and valued brands by understanding and designing for a wide range of consumers and their unique needs, which broadens our reach and helps drive growth.

Ariel Platinum Pods

Ariel SECURECLIC® box is the first Ariel laundry pack to be both certified child-safe and designed to be inclusive and intuitive to use for all adults, thanks to its ergonomic opening system. SECURECLIC® also features the NaviLens code, which can be scanned with a phone to locate the product on the shelf and learn more about it.

Herbal Essences Tactile Markings

Herbal Essences added tactile markings—raised stripes to shampoo bottles and raised circles to conditioner bottles —to help consumers differentiate between the two, making hair care easier for people with low to no vision. The brand continues to incorporate principles of inclusive design into everyday products and innovations to make them easier to use.

Accessible Oral Care with Oral-B

Oral-B established a partnership with the International Association of Disability and Oral Health as part of its work to improve the oral care experience by making it more inclusive and accessible for people with disabilities, their caregivers, and all consumers.

Creating Accessible Advertising

We’re ensuring that people with disabilities not only are accurately represented in our advertising, but also can access our ads, leveraging inclusive-by-design creatives, audio description, and captioning.

Audio Description

We’ve introduced audio description tracks across the majority of our ads in the U.S., UK, and Spain, enabling approximately 30 million blind or visually impaired consumers to understand the video content just by listening.

Real Life Depictions

Through our brands such as Pantene and Oral-B as well as initiatives such as #Shift20, we’re helping to widen the view of people with disabilities, showcasing their strength, resilience, and talent throughout our advertising.
